Amazon uses a variety of aptitude assessment tests. These may include:

  • Numerical Reasoning tests:
    •       ->The Amazon Numerical Reasoning Test measures your ability to make correct decisions 
        from the numerical or statistical data given. You will be expected to use the information 
        presented in tables, graphs and charts to select one of the 5 responses provided.
  • Verbal Reasoning tests:
    •       ->The Amazon Verbal Reasoning Test evaluates your ability to understand and respond to
        questions about information provided in a text. You will be asked to read a given passage
        before considering statements corresponding to the passage.
      ->Your task will then be to decide whether each statement is true, false, or unspecified.
  • Logical Reasoning tests:
    •       ->The Amazon Logical Reasoning Test determines your logical reasoning ability using the 
        information provided in sequences of diagrams containing various shapes. You will be required
        to look at the diagrams and identify underlying rules and patterns in the sequence in order to
        identify the correct diagram that should come next from a list of options.
    

         Hone your communication efficiency and effectiveness.

       ->The Versant Test evaluates your ability to communicate efficiently in the English language. 
         It also tests your use of correct grammar and advanced vocabulary.

       ->The Versant Test is divided into 5 separate parts:
  • Part A: Typing:
    •         You will be given a passage of text and asked to type as many words as possible within a 60 second 
        time allowance.At the end both accuracy and speed are evaluated.
        Tip: You can practice this part of the assessment by opening news articles and typing as much of the 
        text as possible within a 60 second timeframe, checking your accuracy afterwards.
  • Part B: Complete the Sentence:
    •         You will be given 25 seconds to read a short sentence and insert one word only in order to correct the 
        sentence grammatically while matching the context.
      ->Your task will then be to decide whether each statement is true, false, or unspecified.
  • Part C: Dictation:
    •         You will listen to a series of sentences and will then be given 25 seconds to type what you have heard.
  • Part D: Reconstruct the Paragraph:
    •         You will have 30 seconds to read an unknown paragraph, after which the passage is removed and you have 
        90 seconds to reconstruct and type the paragraphâ€™s meaning.
  • Part E: Email Writing:
    •         For the last exercise of the Versant Test you must write a short email of about 100 words according to
        the specific purpose given.
